Why keep an eye on the water?

Rivers and seas should be playgrounds, not health gambles. But companies are releasing untreated waste straight into waterways at an unprecedented rate. Combined with agricultural runoff and micro-plastics, it is harming wildlife and human health alike. Dirty Water is here to help you navigate these waters safely.

Our map layers live discharge alerts so you can decide whether today’s a splash-about day or a stay-on-shore day.

Health & Safety Tips

  • 72-hour rule: After heavy rain or a recorded overflow, wait three days before swimming—most pathogens decline sharply in that window.
  • Spot the signs: Murky water, unusual odours, or floating debris mean step away (and let us know via the report form).

  • Cover cuts: Open wounds are VIP entrances for bacteria. Waterproof plasters are your friends.
  • Shower afterwards: A quick rinse removes potential pathogens before they reach eyes, ears, or mouth.
  • Mind the pups: Dogs love water but can ingest nasties—carry fresh drinking water and towel them down post-splash.

Information provided is for general guidance. If you feel unwell after water contact, ring NHS 111.
